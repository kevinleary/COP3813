Memorial Day 5/25/18 Fishing Trip

A trip to the reef in Tropical Storm Alberto

Posted by Kevin Leary on May 27, 2018

My father and I have been avid fishermen since the very beginning. We went out on our 33' Grady White from Key Colony Beach, FL and went about 30 feet out. What we used to catch The Yellowtail Snappers. We also got one sing Bonita Tuna.

Tackle and Bait

10lb test, Yellow or White weight Jig head hooks, and Penn spinner reels on mojo offshore rods. We used a chum bag tied to the side of the boat to slowly let out chum. Then we sprinkled corn along with that to give more consistent clouds of food for the snappers. If its a good slick with the right tide then I will free-spool my line out with a silverside attached.

Numbers for the Banner day

We ended up getting about 20 more than keeper fish. The regulations on Yellowtail Snappers in Florida is 12" to the end of the tail. All of our keepers were over 13" and our biggest was 16". My dad also was able to snag this nice and good fighting Bonita.

The Bonita (Tuna)

All in all it was an excellent day and A great way to start the season
